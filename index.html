External factors

Variables were extracted from the 2019 economic census on expenses and income for different regions of the country, specifically in the states and municipalities most affected by the passage of Hurricane Hernán in 2020, and that are prior to the environmental phenomenon, in order to identify the causes of inequality and injustice to the most vulnerable communities in these areas.
The variables include data on paid and unpaid personnel, who depend on a social reason or are engaged in informal work, educational and health services to which they have access, detailing the type of health service, the type of economic activity in each State, emphasizing mainly rural industries and predominant commerce in areas with fewer opportunities.



Sentiment analysis

In the following visualizations, a graphic analysis is presented based on the tweets extracted from the hashtag (#) mentioning Hurricane Hernán (#huracanHernan) this in order to know the opinion of the citizens, submitting these tweets to an analysis of feelings to observe the feelings of the population.
A map visualization is also presented to identify where the tweet originated from along with data such as sentiment and text. To measure the impact of this analysis, we also show the number of retweets extracted, that is, how many times the same publication has been shared, and the number of tweets analyzed.
